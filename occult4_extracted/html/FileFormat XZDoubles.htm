XZDoubles.dat

This file contains all the double star information used by OCCULT. The file uses a record length of 200, with the following field definitions. All data is stored as ASCII.

Bytes Format Definition
0 - 6 xxxxxx_ XZ number of the primary star in the pair
7 - 13 xxxxxx_ XZ number of the secondary star in the pair
14 - 19 xxxxxx XZ number of the principle star in the system
20MFlag to indicate whether the XZ80Q position is a mean position. M = mean.
21 - 25 hhmmsss RA (2000) in WDS format
26 - 30 +ddmm Declination (2000) in WDS format
31 - 38 _xxxxxxx ID of double For OCC stars:
  • OCC = definite double
  • OCc=possible double
  • OC*= not a double
    • 39 - 44 _xxxxx Component identifier
    45 - 49 _yyyy Year for first observation
    50 - 54 _yyyy Year for last observation
    55 - 57 _nn Number of observations
    58 - 63 _PPPP.P Position Angle for first observation
    64 - 69 _PPPP.P Position Angle for last observation
    70 - 77 _ddd.ddd Separation for first observation
    78 - 85 _ddd.ddd Separation for last observation
    86 - 91 _mm.mm Magnitude of primary in the pair
    92 - 97 _mm.mm Magnitude of secondary in the pair
    98 -107 _xxxxxxxxx Spectrum (usually of primary)
    108-118 _+xxx +xxx Proper motion (arcsecs/1000 years) in RA & Dec
    119-126 +ddnnnnn BD number
    127-130 _xxxx Note code from WDS
    131-141 _xxxx.xxxxx Period (years)
    142-151 _xx.xxxxxxSemi-major axis (arcsecs)
    152-160 _xxx.xxxx Inclination (deg.)
    161-169 _xxx.xxxx Node (deg)
    170-180 _yyyy.yyyy T0 - date of periastron (years)
    181-188 _0.xxxxx Eccentricity
    189-197 _xxx.xxxx Periastron (deg.)
    198-199 CR/LF sequence

    The meaning of the codes in Notes (columns 128-130) are as follows:

    D Delta Magnitude given.
    N Notes found in the Notes table of WDS.
    O Orbit. Where motion has exceeded 360 degrees, no values are listed in the position angle and separation columns. Orbits may be found in the "Fourth Catalog of Orbits of Visual Binary Stars" (Worley and Heintz, 1983), and in the Information Circulars published by Commission 26 of the IAU, as well as in the literature.
    P 100 year proper motion in right ascension.
    Q 100 year proper motion in declination.
    R 100 year proper motion in right ascension and declination.
    a Pair appears in an appendix list, not part of the discoverer's regular numbering system.
    p Coordinates and proper motions from the ACRS, PPM, IRS, and FK5 catalogs.
    r Pair was listed, but in a "rejected" list, not part of the discoverer's regular numbering sequence.
    s Pair has other discoverer's designations and numbers in the literature due to duplicate discovery. We have given credit to the earliest discovery observation (and correct identification) reported in the literature. The List of Synonyms follows the List of Additional Discoverers.
