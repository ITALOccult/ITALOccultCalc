Satellite positions

This option provides predictions of the location of satellites relative to the planet. The satellites included are:
PlanetSatellites
MarsPhobos, Deimos
JupiterIo, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto, Amalthea, Himalia, Elara, Pasiphae, Sinope, Lysithea, Carme, Ananke, Leda, Thebe, Adrastea, Metis
SaturnMimas, Enceladus, Tethys, Dione, Rhea, Titan, Hyperion, Iapetus, Phoebe
UranusAriel, Umbriel, Titania, Oberon, Miranda, Cordelia, Ophelia, Bianca, Cressida, Desdemona, Juliet, Portia, Rosalind, Belinda, Puck
NeptuneTriton, Nereid
PlutoCharon, Nix, Hydra, Kerberos, Styx

The prediction requires selecting the planet (using the radio buttons), and checking the moons for the prediction - all at the top of the form. Then set the date for the start of the prediction; and for an ephemeris, set the step interval and duration.

The meaning of the output is:
ColumnMeaning
Yr Mth Dy Hrthe geocentric date/time for the prediction
h m sJ2000 RA
o ' ''J2000 declination
MagVisual magnitude. For Iapetus, the orbital phase component is included
Sep"The separation in arc secs from the planet (strictly, from the enter of the planet). [For separations greater than 1 or 2 arc mins, the RA and Dec should be used in preference.]
PAThe J2000 Position angle of the satellite from the Planet
"/minThe motion of the satellite relative to the J2000 star background, in arcsec per minute.
PAThe direction of motion of the satellite .

The ephemerides of all satellites covered by Occult are 'fully accurate', with the single exception of Saturn IX Phoebe (where errors of an arc min are to be expected.). The source of the ephemerides is listed here
