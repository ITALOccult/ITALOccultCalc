Moon Phases, Moon Perigee/Apogee, Physical ephemeris

The date and time of the phases New Moon, First Quarter, Full Moon, and Last Quarter, are computed for the whole year. The time is given to the nearest tenth of an hour. The phase is computed on a geocentric basis, which is the usual basis for identifying the phase of the moon. Note that if the phase of the moon is to be determined by topocentric conditions, the time can differ from the geocentric value by up to about an hour.

The date and time when the moon is at perigee and apogee are similarly computed for the whole year. The time is given to the nearest hour.

The physical ephemeris of the moon is computed at a 2-day interval for 0hrs UT. The values generated are:
llongitude libration (degrees) - geocentric value
blatitude libration (degrees) - geocentric value
AxisPosition angle of the lunar north pole (C) - geocentric value
ColnThe co-longitude of the sun. This corresponds to the selenocentric longitude of the sun-rise terminator.
LatThe selenocentric latitude of the sun
%IllumPercent illumination of the moon
The data is presented in 2 blocks, each covering 6 months.
