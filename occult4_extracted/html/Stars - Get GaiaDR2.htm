Get Gaia DR2 position from VizieR

This form is provided to obtain Gaia DR2 star positions for stars irrespective of magnitude. It is called from the Asteroid Observations editor.

Where the user has the star catalogue files Gaia14_DR2 or Gaia11_DR2, and the user retrieves a star position, occult will retrieve a Gaia DR2 position from those files - assuming the star is present in Gaia DR2 and the star's magnitude is brighter than the faint limit of these files (14 and 11 respectively)

This form allows the user to retrieve Gaia DR2 data irrespective of the star's magnitude. The form has the following appearance:

Identifying the star

The retrieval of the Gaia position is based on the star's coordinates There are two ways of specifying the coordinates:

  1. Manually enter the coordinates in the RA and Dec boxes at the top-right of the form; or
  2. retrieve coordinates from a local copy of UCAC4 or USNO-B1, using the one and Sequence number fields at top left, and clicking the button underneath

When this form is activated from the Asteroid Observations Editor, the UCAC4 or USNO-B1 fields are populated (when available) from the editor.

Where the star coordinates are specified using the star identification at top left, the coordinates specified for the search have had proper motion to 2015.5 applied (2015.5 being the epoch of Gaia DR2). Ifyou are manually entering the star coordinates, you should try to ensure the coordinates you specify are for 2015.5, and not 2000.

Retrieving the Gaia DR2 coordinates

To retrieve the Gaia DR2 coordinates, click the Get Gaia DR2... button at the top right. It will take several seconds or more to send the query to VizieR and retrieve the response. VizieR has 9 sites around the world, and you can select which site you want to use from the menu item Vizier site.... [However some VizieR sites do not have a full collection of catalogues.]

The search conducted by this form retrieves all stars within 4 arc sec of the specified position. This may result in more than one star being returned. Up to 4 stars will be listed, sorted by distance from that position.

Selecting the star

If you have used a reliable source of the star's position to specify its coordinates at 2015.5, the relevant Gaia DR2 entry should be the first listed entry.

However you should scan through the list to make sure you have correctly specified the relevant star. Have particular regard to the magnitudes. Also, whether proper motion has been specified (a star with proper motion specified is more likely to be the relevant star than one with no proper motions). The character sequence NaN indicates no proper motion value.

To select a star, check the corresponding radio button to its left. The first radio button is fixed to 'none' - to provide an option where no coordinates are returned.

Returning the star coordinates

Once you have checked the relevant radio button, click Return selected star. You will be taken back to the Observations editor, where the star's position for the date of the event (corrected for proper motion and parallax) will be displayed.
