Asteroid Multilocation predictions

This form is used to generate local predictions for an event, including the probability of the event occurring.

The list is generated using a site file of your choice which is selected at the top of the form. The site file is retained between sessions.

The meaning of the various items is:
columnComment
Uncertainty in timeThe uncertainty in the prediction time corresponding to the major axis of the error ellipse (which is usually aligned with the direction of motion of the asteroid)
km [or mi]This column gives the observer's distance from the central path, in km, or miles (see below). Note - the calculation does not correct for the curvature of the Earth, and is therefore only approximate for distance in excess of several 100 km.

Positive distances are to the right of the path, when viewed along the direction of motion of the shadow along the path.
dThe geographic direction of the site from the asteroid path.
IMPORTANT. For occultation paths near the pole, or at low altitudes, the direction given here may be incorrect.
ProbabilityThe computed probability that an occultation will be seen at that location. The probability is derived from the uncertainties in the catalogue position and proper motion of the star, and the ephemeris uncertainty in the Asteroid.
LocationThe name of the relevant site.

In addition to sites in the site file, entries are also included for the central line, the northern and southern limits, and those limits as expanded by the uncertainty in the path location.

Longitude, Latitude and AltitudeThe coordinates of the site location. See below.
UTThe predicted time of closest approach at the location. If that time is in the day prior to the UT date in the header, the time is preceded by '-'. If it is in the day following, it is preceded with a '+'.
SeparationThe predicted minimum apparent separation of the star and asteroid at the location
AltThe altitude of the star at the predicted time
SunThe altitude of the sun at the predicted time

Following the table is a line giving the date of calculation of the occultation elements used to generate the predictions. This is very useful if there is a last-minute update to the prediction as it allows you to ascertain whether the prediction is based on the latest updated prediction.

Controlling output

Number and range of sites

You can control the inclusion of sites in the list using the Set distance limits menu item, and then selecting to limit output to sites to those within the 1, 2, or 3 -sigma distances. Or you can apply no restriction and list all sites (which is generally not desirable). As a general guide, you should choose either the 2- or 3- sigma distance. Note that you can retain the distance setting using the specific menu item to save the limit values as a default.

km or miles

You can choose whether the distances are displayed in km or miles under the menu item Set distance limits.

Displayed coordinate precision

The multilocation list is intended for dissemination to observers prior to an event - such as via the internet. This can give rise to privacy concerns (which can vary between legal jurisdictions as well as individuals) For this reason the output of the site coordinates can be either at a precision of 0.1 arc mins, or rounded to the nearest arcmins ending in 5. This is set under the Set distance limits menu item. The default setting is arcmins ending in 5 (to avoid accidental publication of accurate coordinates). NOTE. The calculations are done on the basis of the actual site location, not the displayed rounded site location.
