Phenomena included in the Diary are:

  • conjunctions of the moon and planets with each other, and with the stars Aldebaran, Pollux, Regulus, Spica, and Antares. Separation at conjunction must be less than 6 degrees. Lunar occultations are indicated if one occurs somewhere on the earth.
  • perigee, apogee, and phases of the moon, and dates when moon has its greatest declination north or south
  • eclipses (solar and lunar); transits of Mercury and Venus
  • perihelion, aphelion, equinoxes, and solstices of the Earth. [Note - the precise time of perihelion/aphelion is relatively indeterminate, with the solar distance varying by only a few km over a period of 24hours. The date given in Occult may differ from other sources by up to a day, due to some simplifications incorporated in this part of the program.]
  • opposition, solar conjunction, and stationary points of the planets
  • closest distance to Mars
  • greatest elongation of Mercury and Venus
