Asteroid Light curve data

Light curve data is extracted from the Asteroid Light Curve Database. The entire database may be accessed through the Collaborative Asteroid Lightcurve link CALL . However Occult accesses the data by maintaining a downloaded copy of one of the files in the database - LC_Summary_Pub.txt. Users should update this file from the Downloads page every few months...

The informnation extracted from the Light curve database is:

  • the albedo (for information only)
  • the period of rotation; and
  • the amplitude of the light curve.
The period of rotation is also converted into the angular rotation that will occur in a period of 10 minutes, as well as the time take for the asteroid to rotate 5 deg. If the observations extend over a period whereby the asteroid will have rotated more than 5 degrees, careful consideration about the effects of that rotation on the alignment of the observed chords will be required.

The maximum amplitude of the light curve is also converted into an equivalent ratio of the major:minor axes. As a general proposition, an observed profile should not be significantly more elliptic.

The minimum amplitude of variation is also given - for information only. It is not present for all entries. Most importantly it does not place any constraint on the expected ellipticity of any fit.

Codes

The following codes may appear at the start of the line giving the period of rotation:
CodeExplanation
< Less than.
> Greater than.
D No numerical value.
S Sideral period, default is no flag and synodic period.
U Uncertain, not the same as ambiguous where one or additional periods are reported. For example, the data did not allow finding a definite period and so the author(s) reported a "best guess.".
