J6.bin to J13.bin

The files J6 to J13contain Chebychev polynomials for the Jovian moons 6 to 13. The polynomials were derived by Paris Observatory on a fit to observations made from discovery to 1993. The polynomials are valid for the period 1900 Jan 1 to 2048 October.

The Chebychev polynomials represent a Poisson fit to a numerical integration. An explanation of the data is available here, and the source files in ASCII can be obtained here, as files j6.xyz to j13.xyz.

The series are stored as doubles, seriatum corresponding to the ASCII layout as follows:

  • JD at start of block
  • No of days in block
  • S0, S1, S2 and S3
  • No of lines for power ^0
  • for each line, coeff of Cos(Arg), coeff of Sin(Arg), Arg
  • No of lines for power ^1
  • for each line, coeff of Cos(Arg), coeff of Sin(Arg), Arg
  • No of lines for power ^2
  • for each line, coeff of Cos(Arg), coeff of Sin(Arg), Arg
This structure repeats for each coordinate (x, y, z), and then for each block.
