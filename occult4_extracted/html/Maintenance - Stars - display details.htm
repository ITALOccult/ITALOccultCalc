Stars - display details

This allows the user to read the star catalogue information used by OCCULT. The catalogues that can be displayed fall into three groups, as follows:

  1. Stars that are in the ecliptic. That is, ZC, XZ, and SAO stars close to the ecliptic
  2. Stars in the main catalogues used for asteroidal occultation predictions. That is, stars with Hipparcos, Tycho-2 and UCAC4 numbers, as well as Gaia stars identified by a coordinate ID
  3. Stars in various other catalogues that have been used for asteroidal occultation predictions, which are generally out-of-date. Assuming the catalogues have been installed, they are UCAC4, UCAC3, UCAC2, URAT1, PPMXL, NOMAD and USNO-B1

When entering a Gaia star by coordinate identification, the format is: 
    hhmmss.s+ddmmss
All spaces must be filled. For example an RA of 1h 3m 0.5s is specified as 010300.5

Checking any of the catalogues under the Asteroidal heading will result in Hipparcos, Tycho2 and UCAC4 positions being retrieved from Gaia DR2 wherever possible.

If the form is called from the Right-click menu in lunar occultation predictions, the form will open with the relevant star displayed. Otherwise, select the relevant catalogue, enter the star number, then click 'Display details'.

The catalogue information displayed is:

  • The star catalogue numbers, and any names
  • J2000 Right Ascension and annual proper motion, epoch if it is not 2000.0, and Gaia catalogue (if sourced from Gaia)
  • J2000 Declination, and annual proper motion in declination, epoch if it is not 2000.0
  • Available magnitudes, spectral type (ZC, SAO and XZ, parallax (Hipparcos only), and the angular diameter (details) (all but UCAC2 stars).
  • An option to display entries in the Washington Double Star catalog (WDS), the Interferometric Catalog, and the AAVSO Index file of variable stars, within about 2 arcmins of the star position. If the star is a variable star, the AAVSO light curve (if one exists) can be obtained from here. (details)

The remaining information is only available for stars in the XZ catalogue

If the star is a variable star, basic variable star information is presented. Variable type is as per the General Catalogue of Variable Stars.
