Positions of the Planets

The Positions menu provides for the computation of the geocentric ephemeris of the Sun and the major planets for any date. The basis of the ephemeris depends on the files downloaded for the installation, and the date of the ephemeris. The JPL DE440 ephemeris or subsequent versions is used whenever possible, which involves an extra download from the basic Occult download (Download 33 or 34).

Positions may be computed on three different reference frames
Ref frameMeaning
J2000 standard equinoxGeocentric position on the mean equinox of J2000 equinox. Such positions can be plotted directly onto star charts drawn for that equinox.
Mean Equinox of dateGeocentric apparent positions on the mean equinox of date. That is, the position has been precessed to the date, but no nutation has been applied.
Apparent equinoxGeocentric apparent positions on the apparent equinox of date. That is, precession and nutation have been applied.

Predictions are computed for the months specified, and at an interval of days as specified.  The predictions are given to a nominal high precision, but can be listed at a lower precision by clearing the High precision check box.

The data produced in the ephemeris is:
Right Asc.right ascension, in hours, min, sec.
Declinationdeclination, in deg, min, sec
Distancethe geocentric distance to the planet, in AU
diathe planet's apparent equatorial diameter, in arc sec
magthe apparent magnitude of the planet
Elongthe planet's elongation in degrees, from the sun - with direction east or west being indicated
Ithe phase angle of illumination (in degrees) - i.e. the sun-planet-earth angle
%Illthe percent illumination of the planet
Limbthe Position Angle (in degrees) of the mid-point of the illuminated limb of the planet
Dethe planetocentric declination of the Earth. For Saturn, this is also the apparent inclination of the rings. The sign indicates whether the northern or southern pole is inclined towards the Earth. [This is not the latitude of the sub-Earth point]
Ppthe position angle of the north pole of the planet.
Dsthe planetocentric declination of the Sun. The sign indicates whether the northern or southern pole is inclined towards the Earth. For Saturn, when De and Ds have opposite signs, the un-illuminated side of the rings is visible - and 'rings unlit' is indicated
