Star diameters

Star diameters are displayed in several locations in Occult. The basis of the values displayed is:

    • The apparent angular diameter for stars in the Occult Gaia subset files. This value has been derived from:
      • gaiadr3.Astrophysical_parameters_supp file, field radius_flame_spec,  (Rad-Flame in VizieR), and 
      •  gaiadr3.Astrophysical_parameters file, field radius_flame,  (Rad-Flame in VizieR).
      with the solar radius value having been converted to the apparent star diameter with:
            diam(mas)= 9.304 * parallax(asec) * radius_flame
    • If a measured value exists in the CHARM2 or CADARS catalogues with a value that is greater than 1 mas,  that measured value.

  • If the star position is not obtained from the Gaia subset files (for example, positions from PPMX, USNO-B1, NOMAD, and User stars) the star diameter will be the value listed in the CHARM2 or CADARS catalogues. Diameters derived using the Warner expression (see below) are no longer displayed.
Occult will list the diameter in two formats:
  • 0.xxxx" [Estimated] - which indicates that the diameter has been derived from the Gaia Flame values; or:
  • 0.xxxx" [CHARM/CADARS, y measures] - which indicates the diameter is a measured diameter extracted from one or both of the CHARM2 and CADARS catalogues. The value of "y" gives number of measures that have been averaged to provide the indicated diameter.

Prior to diameters being derived from the Gaia Flame values, the following was used to estimate a star's diameter from its color magnitudes

The diameter is computed on the basis of the Warner expression. That is :

  • An estimate of the diameter derived from the star magnitude and colour. The expression used is:
    StarDia = Math.Pow(10, 0.73 * (B-V) - 0.2 * Mv - 2.512);
    where:
    • StarDia is the angular diameter in arc secs
    • V is the Mv magnitude; and
    • B is the Mp magnitude.

  • If Mp is not available, and Mr is available, V-R is converted to B-V using:
    (B-V) = (V-R)/0.57
    [see http://brucegary.net/photometry/V-R.htm ]
