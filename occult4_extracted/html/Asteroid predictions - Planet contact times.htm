  • The prediction is generated for all sites contained in the site file selected at the top of the form. To generate predictions for your home location, merely select a site file containing that location.
  • A site will only be included in the prediction if at least one event occurs at that site. If an event occurs with the Sun above the horizon, or the planet below the horizon, the time is indicated as '.. ....'.
  • The ring numbers listed do not correspond to the official ring designations. They represent a sequential numbering away from the planet. In the case of Saturn, the 'rings' 1 to 5 represent the inner edge of C, the juncture of C and B, the outer edge of B, the inner edge of A, and the outer edge of A.
  • The accuracy of the prediction will be dependent upon the planet's rate of motion, as well as the effect of planetary atmosphere. The slower the motion in the sky, the greater the uncertainty.
  • Predictions of events involving rings do not take into account whether (at the time of the event) the star is located behind the planet. Of course, if the star is occulted at the time, the ring event will not be visible! The user will need to eliminate all events that occur while the star is occulted by the planet. Ring information is included for Jupiter, Saturn and Uranus.
  • The predictions on this form do not apply for the moons of planets.

    Functions provided

  • the list can be sorted by site name, longitude or latitude, using the menu item Sort.
  • You can control whether the list should be broken at every 5th line under the menu item with Predictions....
  • If the list is to be published, you can limit the precision of the site coordinates displayed in the list, under the menu item with Predictions..... Note that the calculation is always conducted on the basis of the full-precision coordinates.
