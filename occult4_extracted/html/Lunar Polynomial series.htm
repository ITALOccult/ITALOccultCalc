Lunar polynomial series

For convenience of use, Occult generates a polynomial expression valid for a 24hr period, by computing the position of the moon at 0, 4.8, 9.6, 14.4, 19.2 and 24hrs, and fitting a 5th order polynomial to these positions (4th order for parallax.)

An example display of the series is:

Lunar polynomial for 2010 Jul  5

a0    13.9038521   11.6560484   0.91921601
a1    11.5578319    4.6472255   0.00958632
a2       3156473     -1815201       129417
a3        389671      -337959        -4680
a4         -8660       -20013        -1109
a5         -4128         -675

Longitude correction = 0.00"
 Latitude correction = 0.00"

JPL DE423

The 3 columns of numbers correspond to the lunar right ascension, declination, and parallax – all in degrees.

Each line gives the term in the series. ‘a0’ is the constant part of the series; ‘a1’ is the term in the first order of time; ‘a2’ is the term in the second order of time, and so on. Time is measured in fractions of a day, and measured in Terrestrial dynamic time. Thus, to compute a position, evaluate the series as:

a0 +a1*t+a2*t2+a3*t3+a4*t4+a5*t5

The next two lines give the corrections to the lunar ephemeris that have been included. The corrections are zero.
Historical note: corrections used to be applied to refer the ephemeris to the center of figure rather than the center of mass. All computations and limb corrections are now referred to the center of mass - so that these corrections are no longer used.

The final line gives the source of the ephemeris.
