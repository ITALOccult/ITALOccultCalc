Events involving distant asteroids

This list is provided to help identify observed occultations involving distant asteroids.

The criterion that is applied is the heliocentric distance of the asteroid at the time of the occultation. The criterion for inclusion is a heliocentric distance greater than 4 AU - which is at the outer edge of the main asteroid belt - but inside the orbit of Jupiter.

The list can be sorted by heliocentric distance, asteroid name or number, or event date.

When analysing the list, remember that some asteroids move in quite eccentric orbits, and as a result can appear in the list at quite different heliocentric distances. For example, the asteroid 2060 has entries at 9.0 and 12.0 AU.
