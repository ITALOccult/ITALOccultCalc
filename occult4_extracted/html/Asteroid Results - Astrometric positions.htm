Astrometric positions from occultations

This provides a list of astrometric positions derived  from asteroid occultations.

There are 4 options for setting what is displayed, selected at the top of the form:

 

The left box controls whether the data is displayed as at the event time (that is, the average time of the events of all successful observers), or at the time of geocentric conjunction.

The check box List events for a year allows you to limit the list to events from a selected year. The check box List planets only limits the listing to events involving planets and satellites.

Another important set of options is the ability to sort the list by a range of parameters, using the menu item Sort...

  • Asteroid name
  • Asteroid number
  • Source of the star position
  • Date of the event
  • RA offset
  • Dec offset
  • Separation at event
  • Date of conjunction
  • Separation at conjunction
  • Uncertainty in separation

Under the with Positions... menu, various options are provided for copying, printing or saving some or all of the listed events.  These include:

  • Copying just a selection of listed events (& clearing that selection)
  • Copying the entire displayed list
  • Printing the entire displayed list (landscape mode, double-sided if available on the printer)
  • Saving all displayed events, either as text or CSV.

 The displayed list has a nominal time precision of 0.01 sec, and angular precisions of 0.00001sec, 0.0001", and 0.1mas. If the data is saved as a .CSV file, an extra digit of precision is provided.

Explanation of columns

The information provided is:

  • The object number and name. For asteroids the number is the usual asteroid number. For planets it is constructed as PxMyy, where x is the planet number (Mercury to Pluto), and mm is the moon number within the planet's system
  • Year, Month, Day - specifies either the Event Time, or the Time of Geocentric Conjunction. In the case of an entry for an asteroidal satellite, the time of geocentric conjunction is for the relevant body.
  • Object Right Ascension and declination. The derived position of the object, taking into account the effects of differential precession, nutation, aberration and deflection. The position is an astrometric position.
  • The Right Ascension and Declination of the occulted star, whose coordinates are used to determine the coordinates of the object. The position has proper motion to the event date applied, as well as parallax. But gravitational deflection has not been applied. For stars from the Gaia DR2 catalogue that are brighter than mag 12.5, the position is corrected for the frame rotation error in Gaia DR2.
  • Src identifies the star catalogue used for the position. The code is that used by the Minor Planet Center. GDR2 = Gaia Data Release2, Hip2=Hipparcos, Ty2=Tycho2, U4 = UCAC4
  • N The number of observed chords
  • The offset of the object from the star position. For At event, the offset is in RA and Dec, in arcsec. For At conjunction the offset is in separation (arcsec) and Position Angle (J2000) (Degrees).
  • Next there is a group of uncertainty values, with there being an extra two columns in the At event listing
    • The first column gives the  orientation of the track of the asteroid past the star 0 to 180 deg. The along-track and across-track uncertainties relate to this direction. This is particularly relevant to events with insufficient chords to define a best-fit ellipse - as then the along-track uncertainty can be well-defined whereas the across-track uncertainty is poorly defined.
    • Event T is the uncertainty in the time of the event arising from the timing uncertainties reported by the various observers. Where observers have not reported a timing uncertainty, the uncertainty is set as explained here.
    • Next there are a group of columns relating to the uncertainties arising from the fit of the observations to the object.
      • For At event, there are 6 columns as follows:
        • the uncertainty in time (secs)
        • the equivalent uncertainty along-track in mas
        • the uncertainty across-track in mas
        • three columns giving the equivalent uncertainties in RA and Dec, together with their correlation
      • For At conjunction there are 3 columns as follows:
        • the uncertainty in time (secs)
        • the equivalent uncertainty along-track in mas
        • the uncertainty across-track in mas
    • Uncertainties in the star position (RA and Dec) in mas. For Gaia DR2 stars this uncertainty includes the uncertainty from the Frame rotation correction.
  • Finally there are three flags, as follows:
    • The first flag is for whether the Gaia Duplicate Source flag is set. It is an indication of possible duplicity, and consequential unreliability of the position;
    • the Revised Unit Weight Estimate  (RUWE) - a Gaia value relating to the reliability of the astrometry. The flag is set if the RUWE value indicates there is cause for concern
    • A flag to indicate that the reported position is the center of a Shape Model that has been fitted to the observations.
