Binary asteroid - estimate orbit size or Period

This for provides an estimate of either the Period of a binary asteroid, or the Orbit Radius, given the approximate sizes of the asteroid and its satellite, and the assumed density of the bodies.

The method involves solving the following equation for either a (the orbit radius), or P (the orbital period), using a value of the Mass of the system M

a3 = G * P2 * M /(4 * π 2)

where G = 6.67259 x 10-11 , with the parameters specified in units of centimeters for length, kg for weight, seconds for time, and grams/cc for density.

The parameter M is the combined mass of the two bodies. For convenience, the Mass is determined by specifying the diameter of  the two bodies, and specifying an estimated density of the material making up the bodies.

 

Size of the bodies

At the top of the form, in the first two fields, specify the diameters of the main body, and the satellite. (If the diameter of the satellite is less than about 20% of the main body, its Mass is too small to make a significant difference in the result)

 

Density of the system

In the 3rd field, you can specify your estimate of the density of the system. The output area below includes the results for your estimate, and 8 other density values. Three of those have associated text setting out a description of the Asteroid Class to which the particular density value is appropriate

 

Solving for Period, or Orbit Radius

The last two fields are used to set either :

  • the assumed Orbital Period of the system, in hours; or
  • the assumed Orbit Radius of the system, in km

Below those is a table that gives, for a range of Density values, the corresponding orbital radius, or orbital period. The color of the fields corresponds to the color of assumed parameter that has been set. The first value in the table corresponds to the user-set density of the system.
