Summary of changes made since version 4.1.0.0

Occult 4.1.3.1 is based on a number of incremental additions or changes, as well as numerous bug fixes.The following is a summary of notable changes introduced into Occult since version 4.1.0.0.

General changes

  • Added 'Strong Signing' to provide better security against hackers
  • Incorporated a new accurate ephemeris of Phoebe (Saturn IX), plus ephemerides for the Lagrangian satellites Helene (Saturn XII), Telesto (Saturn XIII) and Calypso (Saturn (XIV).
  • Updated the orbits of Pluto's satellites Charon, Hydra and Nix, and added orbits for Kerberos and Styx.
  • Added functionailty to access the latest JPL-DE ephemerides as they become available
  • Star catalogues - UCAC4. Added a check of whether the installed copy of UCAC4 has been corrected for certain errors.
  • Implemented the IMCCE L1.2 theory of the Galilean satellites.

    Asteroid predictions

  • added functionality to display rings around asteroids
  • Added Kepler2 target stars
  • Added unctionality to convert TNO occultation predictions generated by the IMCCE & RIO group, to Occult format.

    Asteroid observations/analysis

    * Added AOTA - Asteroidal Occultation Time Analyser

    Lunar predictions

  • Added Kepler2 target stars
  • For occultations of double stars, added an estimate of the time difference between the primary and secondary star.

    Lunar observations

  • Lunar occultation observations editor. Added a warning when the graze flag is set for the current entry and the previous entry - if the stars are different - to prevent inadvertant failure to clear the flag for a non-graze event.

    Eclipses

  • Lunar Eclipse predictions. Contact times generated using the Danjon method using the latest determination of the height of the notional eclipse-forming layer - 86.9km. Also the umbral shadow is adjusted to allow for the oblateness of the Earth as seen from the declination of the Sun.
  • Added code to reduce Lunar Crater Timings made during a lunar eclipse
  • Solar eclipses, Lunar eclipses, transits. Revised the way that deltaT was handled with predictions. Incorporated display of uncertainty in the value of deltaT
  • Solar eclipses. Revision of the plotting routines, to eliminate a range of plotting errors. Added a display of the elongations of the major planets at the time of the eclipse.
  • Solar eclipses. Added functionality to identify eclipses where planets are occulted or transiting the sun at the time of the eclipse, or within one degree of the sun.
  • Lunar eclipses. Added functionality to identify eclipses where planets are nearby, and potentially occulted by, the eclipsed moon.
  • Transits. Improved the functionality for finding events in historical periods.

    Ephemerides

  • Added functionality to display rings around asteroids
