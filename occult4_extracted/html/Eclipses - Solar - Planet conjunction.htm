Planet conjunctions at Eclipses

This form is used to find planets close to the Sun at a solar eclipse, or close to the Umbra for a lunar eclipse. The form is called from either the Solar eclipse main form, or the Lunar Eclipse form, under the menu item Find close planetary conjunctions.

Solar Eclipses

When called from the Solar Eclipse form, this finds eclipses where one or more of the major planets are within 1 degree of the Sun, or (more particularly) are either occulted by or transitting the sun at the time of the eclipse. The form looks like:

To identify relevant eclipses, specify the start and end year for the search. The up-down controls for specifying the year changes the year in increments of 100 years. The allowable year range is -13000 to +17000 - the range covered by the DE431 ephemeris. Then click Find eclipses. A progress bar will appear to indicate progress. The routine works by accessing the normal prediction capabilites; this results in the regular display of a world-map for an eclipse - being the first eclipse in a 10-year block as the program works through the date range.

For each event, the list will identify all planets within 1 degree of the sun, together with a flag for each planet that indicates the nature of the event. [Most events involve only one planet.] The flag has the following meanings:
FlagMeaning
{none}Planet is within 1 degree of the sun. However it is not close enough to be occulted by, or to be transiting, the sun.
{sun}Planet is closer than 18 arc mins to the center of the sun. Depending on the separation and the radius of the sun at the time, the planet might be occulted by the sun.
{smiley}Planet is closer than 18 arc mins to the center of the sun. Depending on the separation and the radius of the sun at the time, the planet might be in transit over the sun.

The usual eclipse predictions can be generated by simply clicking on an event in the list. All the usual prediction options for that event are then available.

The list can be copied, printed or saved under the with List menu option.

Lunar Eclipses

When called from the Lunar Eclipse form, this finds eclipses where one or more of the major planets are within 3 degrees of the umbral shadow axis, with an indication of whether an occultation of the planet by the moon is possible. The form looks like:

To identify relevant eclipses, specify the start and end year for the search. The up-down controls for specifying the year changes the year in increments of 100 years. The allowable year range is -13000 to +17000 - the range covered by the DE431 ephemeris. Then click Find eclipses. A progress bar will appear to indicate progress. The routine works by accessing the normal prediction capabilites; this results in the regular display of a world-map for an eclipse - being the first eclipse in a 10-year block as the program works through the date range.

For each event, the list will identify all planets within 3 degrees of the axis of the umbral shadow (as seen from the geocenter), together with a flag that indicates whether a lunar occultation of the planet is possible. The displayed distances are referenced to the axis of the umbral shadow as seen from the Geocenter. The flag has the following meanings:
FlagMeaning
{none}All listed planets are within 3 degrees of the umbral axis, but none are close enough to be occulted by the moon.
{sun}One or more of the planets listed are closer to the umbral axis than the umbral radius plus the lunar parallax (about 1.5 to 1.8 deg), such that the planet is located within the umbral shadow as seen from some parts of the earth. The planet can be identified by it being followed by a † {dagger} symbol.
{smilie}One or more of the planets can be occulted by the moon as seen from somewhere on the Earth - AND it is located within the umbral shadow as seen from some parts of the Earth. As a result, it is likely that a lunar occultation of that planet, with the planet being located in the umbral shadow, is possible from somewhere on the Earth. The planet can be identified by the separation being given in arc-minutes, and by it being followed by a ‡{double-dagger} symbol.

The usual eclipse predictions can be generated by simply clicking on an event in the list. All the usual prediction options for that event are then available.

The list can be copied, printed or saved under the with List menu option.
