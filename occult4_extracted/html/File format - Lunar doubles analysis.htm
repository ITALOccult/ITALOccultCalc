File format - Lunar observations - double star analysis

The form for analysing lunar occultations for double star details allows the data to be saved and loaded. The default location for all such files is Occult 4\Observations\Doubles. The data in the file is as follows.

Line 1 contains identification details. The data is not used in the analysis, but does form the basis of he file name.
ColumnsData
0 - xstar identification
x+2 to x+5Year
x+7 to x+8Month
x+10 to x+11Day

Line 2 contains fixed header text.

The data lines contain:
ColumnsData
1 - 10The date of the event. Format is yyyy mm dd
13 - 28The name of the observer (T1)
30 - 35event seconds of the time, brighter star (T1)
36 - 43event seconds of the time, fainter star (T2). The zero point is the start of the minute for (T1)
44 - 50T2-T1, seconds
52 - 58PA of the event
60 - 66rv - the radial rate of motion of the star relative to the lunar limb
68 - 74cct - angle relative to the normal
76 - 82slope - the 'slope' of the lunar limb between the points of contact of the primary and secondary star, in degrees. A positive slope corresponds to the limb hight decreasing as the Axis Angle increases. slope is calculated from the Primary_AA, Secondary_AA, and the respective heights.
85* - a flag to indicate whether the event is included in the solution. '1' = included; ' ' = not included
88 - 92l - topocentric lunar libration in longitude
95 - 99b - topocentric lunar libration in latitude
102 -107AA - topocentric Axis Angle of the primary star at its reported time of occultation
109-113Scale - the ratio of the apparent lunar radius to the mean lunar radius. Used to determine precise limb heights.
115-119°/asec - the re number of degrees around the apparent lunar limb corresponding to 1 arcsec. Used to convert double star separation to a change in the Axis Angle
122-127Primary_AA - the Axis Angle of the primary star at the time of the occultation of the secondary
129-133ht - limb height at the Primary_AA
136-141Second_AA - the Axis Angle of the secondary star at the time of its occultation
143-147ht - limb height at the Second_AA
149-152CA - the Cusp Angle of the event
153-156Ill- the % illumination of the moon
159-160Alt - the altitude of the moon at the event

After the event lines, there is a single blank line, followed by a line containing the solution.

Note 1 The following data items essential for the analysis come from the observer's double star report:

  • Date
  • T1, T2
  • PA
  • RV
  • CCT
  • l
  • b
  • AA
  • Scale
    For 'old' reports that did not include one or all of l, b, AA or Scale, the values are retrieved from the Lunar Archive files.

    The following data items are computed as part of the solution process:

  • slope
  • °/asec
  • Primary_AA, and height at that location
  • Secondary_AA, and height at that location

    The following information come from the observer's double star report:

  • Observer's name
  • CA
  • Ill
  • Alt
    CA, Ill, and Alt are provided to assist in subjectiuve assessment (if needed) of the reliability of an observation.
