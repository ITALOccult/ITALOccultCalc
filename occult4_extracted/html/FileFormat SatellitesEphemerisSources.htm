Satellites - ephemeris sources

The following lists the source of the ephemerides of the satellites of the planets used in Occult.

Mars

  • A.T. Sinclair The orbits of the satellites of Mars determined from Earth-based and spacecraft observations Astron. Astrophys. 220, 321-328 (1989)

    Jupiter

    Satellites I - IV

  • The IMCCE L1 theory of the Galilean satellites. Specifically, Synthetic representation of the Galilean satellites' orbital motions from L1 ephemerides. Fortran source code is available at the IMCCE ftp site.

    Satellites VI-XIII

  • Orbites et Ephemerides des Satellites Lointains de Jupiter, Notes Scientific et Techniques du Bureau des longitudes, S054, June 1997. For the period 1900 Jan 1 to 2048 Oct 31 - the Chebychev polynomials of the full solution, available from the FTP server at Bureau des Longitudes, as files j6.xyz to j13.xyz, are used. Outside this period, the approximate solutions given in the paper are used.

    Satellites V, XIV-XVI

  • Elements have been taken from http://ssd.jpl.nasa.gov/?sat_elem.

    Saturn

    Satellites I - VI, VIII

  • A. Vienne and L. Duriez Tass 1.6: Ephemerides of the major Saturnian Satellites Astron Astrophys. 297, 588-605 (1995)

    Satellite VII

  • L. Duriez and A. Vienne Theory of motion and ephemerides of Hyperion Astron Astrophys. 324, 366-380 (1997) [Note This, together with Tass 1.6, makes Tass 1.7]

    Satellite IX

  • For dates between 1875 July 01 and 2022 Jun 30 - the Chebychev polynomials from Desmars J, Li S.N., Tajeddine R., Peng Q.Y., Tang Z.H. Phoebe's Orbit from ground-based and space-based observations A&A 553, pp A36-A46, (2013). The series has been truncated to the first 7 coeffs in x,y,z. The largest omitted term has a value of 3.7e-10 AU. This file is provided as an optional download (due to its recent availability).
  • Otherwise, R.A. Jacobsen The orbit of Phoebe from Earthbased and Voyager observations Astron Astrophys. Suppl. Ser. 128, 7-17 (1998). [ only gives a mean orbit referenced to the Laplacian plane. Perturbations from P.E. Zadunaisky A determination of new elements of the orbit of Phoebe, ninth satellite of Saturn Astron. J 59, 1-6 (1954) have been included, but errors can approach an arc-min.])

    Satellites XII, XIII, XIV

  • P. Oberti and A. Vienne An upgraded theory for Helene, Telesto, and Calypso, A&A 397, 353–359 (2003)

    Uranus

    Satellites I - V

  • J. Laskar and R.A. Jacobson GUST86. An analytical ephemeris of the Uranian satellites Astron. Astrophys 188, 212-224 (1987)
  • For the period 2006 Jan 1 to 2011 Dec 30, Chebychev polynomials available on the FTP server at the Bureau des Longitudes , as files NOE-coefcheb1.res to NOE-coefcheb5.res, are used.

    Satellites VI - XV

  • R.A. Jacobson The orbits of the inner Uranian satellites from Hubble Space Telescope and Voyager 2 observations Astron J 115, 1195-1199 (1998)

    Neptune

  • Triton

  • N. V. Emelyanov and M. Yu. Samorodov. Triton ephemeris from 'Analytical theory of motion and new ephemeris of Triton from Observations  MNRAS 454, 2205-2215, (2015)

    Nereid

  • R.A. Jacobson, J.E. Riedel, and A.H. Taylor The orbits of Triton and Nereid from spacecraft and Earthbased observations Astron. Astrophys. 247, 565-575 (1991)

    Pluto

  • Marina Brozovic, Mark R. Showalter, Robert A. Jacobson, Marc W. Buie The orbits and masses of satellites of Pluto, Icarus [Preprint, Aug 2014].
  • M W Buie, W M Grundy, E.F Young, L A Young, S A Stern Orbits and photometry of Pluto's satellites: Charon, P/2005 P1, S/2005 P2 [Preprint, 2005 Dec 19]
