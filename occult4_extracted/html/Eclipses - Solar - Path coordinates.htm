Solar eclipse - central line circumstances, and path limits

On this form you can generate the coordinates of the eclipse path. Specify the longitude range, and step interval, at the top of the form, and click Compute.

You can move to the forms for generating multilocation prediction, the local prediction, or the detailed map, using the menu item Detailed predictions.

Explanation of the prediction

The prediction generated provides the following information:

  • Longitude
  • for the Central eclipse:
    • UTC of maximum eclipse. If the event occurs on the day preceding or following that date of the prediction, a '-' or '+' (respectively) occurs immediately after the predicted time.
    • duration of central eclipse (secs); This is followed by 'T' for a total eclipse, and 'A' for an annular eclipse.
    • Magnitude of central eclipse
    • Altitude of the Sun
  • For the Central path
    • Latitude of the lines of central eclipse, the northern limit, and the southern limit, of the central eclipse.
  • For the Penumbral region of the eclipse:
    • Latitude of the northern limit, and southern limit, of the partial eclipse.

Data is not given if the relevant event or limit does not occur at the particular longitude.

[The prediction does not give the limit of eclipse visibility that is defined by the Sun being on the horizon.]
